Music

Useful Information

Music is one of the oldest artforms in history, but making music has never been easier. The digital age has brought studio level sound to the palms of our hands. Here we'll show you some things you might like when getting started.


DAWs

A Digital Audio Workspace or DAW is what you use to create and organize music digitally. They allow you to create music digitally or record music live. Selecting the one for you comes down to personal preference and price. Below are a couple popular DAWs.

FL Studios

This is the most popular DAW, especially for beginners. The main reason being that they offer a free version of their service. The free versions has limitations such as limited instrument selection and the inability to save your project. While not ideal, the free version allows users to dip their toe before making a final purchase. The full versions of FL Studios allow for professional level quality.

Ableton Live

Ableton is less userfriendly than FL Studios, there is also no free version. Ableton is very confusing for new users. People with a strong background in music production would enjoy Ableton.


Garage Band

Garage Band is one of if not the best DAWs, especially for beginners. Garage band is free and extremely powerful. Popular artists such as Rhianna, Steve Lacy, and T-Pain. The only downside of Garage Band is that it's exclusively on Mac/iOS.


Audio Interfaces

An Audio interface is a device that allows you to connect microphones, instruments, or other audio gear to your computer. Audio interfaces improve sound quality, offer lower latency, and provide essenial ports that built-in sound cards often lack. They're almost essential for high quality music production, voice overs, or any other pro audio work.

Behringer U-Phoria UM2

The Behringer U-Phoria UM2 offers 2 channel recording, +48V of phantom power, and headphone output. With input for a microphone and instrument, this interface is a good deal for people looking for their first interface.

Price: $43

Focusrite Scarlett Solo

The Focusrite Scarlett Solo has been one of the most famous audio interfaces for a long time. Features such as Air Mode, Gain Halos, Direct Monitoring, and 24-bit/192 kHz converters allow for studio-quality sound.

Price: $110
