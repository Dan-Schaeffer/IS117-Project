Useful Information

When purchasing a camera it's important to know what the specifications for cameras mean.

First you need to determine what the camera will be used for, some cameras are better for video while some are better for pictures.


When looking at image quality the 2 things to look out for are sensor size, and megapixels.

Bigger Sensors usually mean better quality. Megapixels are important, but after about 20MP they don't matter as much. Unless you're printing huge photos.

It's also important to check out the lense; can the lens be changed? Are their good lenses avaible for the model/brand you're buying.


Check if the camera can record video. If so, check for things like resolution(e.g 4K, 1080p), frame-rates(e.g 24fps for cinematics, 60fps for smooth motion),

microphone input, and flip screen before making a final purchase.

Things like auto focus, low-light performance, battery life, and ease of use are also important to check before deciding on a camera.

Low-End Cameras

Phone

Your phone may be the best option to start with. Any iPhone past the 15 has a 48MP camera. Its ease of use, compatibility and portability make it a good option for beginner creators. Phones also allow for 3rd party hardware such as lenses and microphones. The downsides of using your phone are storage, and wear and tear from daily use.


Camera Quality: 8/10

Audio Quality: 7/10

Skill Level: Beginner

Price: Varies

Usecase: Daily or beginner photography/videography

WD08 Digital Camera

This digital point and shoot camera advertises 4K, 64MP video at 30fps. It's less compact than a phone, but more compact than most other cameras. This camera features a flipable display, 1 hour battery life, 32GB microSD, and a built in microphone. The issue with this camera lies in it's sensor and microphone. There seems to be no external microphone input meaning users must either have poor audio, or record audio on a different device. A small sensor also means that the camera is unable to utilize all 64MP, causing the quality to drop. Cheaper cameras often advertise high megapixels despite having a small sensor.


Camera Quality: 5/10

Audio Quality: 3/10

Skill Level: Beginner

Price: $60-$80

Usecase: Family photos/memories

Mid-Range Cameras

Sony ZV-1

This point and shoot camera was made famous by popular creators on Youtube. This camera offers 20.1MP with a 1" sensor, meaning high quality is attainable. This camera comes with an exterior microphone allowing for higher audio quality out of the box. This compact and portable camera offers an articulating flip screen along with an ultra-wide 20mm lens. The screen allows you to see yourself, and the lens allows you to get everything in frame. This camera allows for different external mics to be used. This camera also offers auto focus up to 24fps.


Camera Quality: 9/10

Audio Quality: 8/10

Skill Level: Beginner

Price: $400

Usecase: Vlogging

Canon EOS M50

This Mirrorless DSLR camera allows users to film 4k video up to 23fps, 1080p 60fps, or 720p 120fps which allows for slow motion. The image quality on this camera is superb, but it's built in audio is somewhat lacking. This camera was designed with an exterior microphone in mind. This camera is also compatible with many different lenses allowing for more diverse shots.

Canon XF605 Camcorder

This product differs from the others because it is strictly a camcorder. Camcorders specialize in video, offering things like better audio and better zoom. This product films in 13.4MP with a 1" 4K UHD sensor. A device like this would be common place on your average film set. With USB, HDMI, ethernet, headphone, and microphone input the Canon XF605 offers a variety of ways to interface and export the footage filmed. It's important to remember that this is a camcorder, not a camera, so it lacks the ability to take photos.
