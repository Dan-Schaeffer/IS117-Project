Microphones

Useful Information

When buying a microphone it's important to remember what and where you'll be using it. The 3 types of devices commonly used with microphones are computers, phones, and cameras. Before buying a microphone it's important to check if it's compatible with your device, if it will suit your needs/environment, and if it requires an additional power source. Most mics that use a standard audio jack or USB allow them to be used with phones using an adapter.The 4 types of microphones we'll cover are Dynamic, Condenser, Shotgun, and Lavalier.


Microphone Types

Dynamic microphones are rugged and durable, meaning they are harder to blowout and harder to break via dropping. They are good for recording live vocals and instruments, they specialize with loud sounds such as a live concert. Since they specialize with loud sounds, to record a normal speaking level, the speaker would have to be very close to the mic.

Condenser microphones are usually more powerful, meaning high end condenser mics require power sources. Condenser mics also specialize with crisp recording of vocals and instruments, but it doesn't handle loud sounds as well as a dynamic mic. This means that condenser mics are better for studio recordings. Famous artists like The Beatles and Frank Sinatra used condenser mics when recording in their studios.

Shotgun microphones are a type of condenser mic, but they are highly directional. This means shotgun mics record sound from wherever they're pointed and attempt to cancel any noise from outside of their scope. Shotgun mics have excellent background noise reduction. Shotgun mics are also used as boom mics.

Lavalier or Lapel mics are small mics that can be attatched to the subject (usually clipped to clothes). Lavalier or Lav mics can be wired or wireless but they're used for whenever the subject of the video is mobile.


Products

Dynamic Mics

FIFINE K688

This microphone is a good budget option. It is dynamic which means it's better for loud sounds or for keeping out background noise. While you would have to be close to the microphone for things like vlogs or podcast, you'd need not worry when recording things like instruments (especially drums).

Price: $50

Shure MV7X

This microphone is our mid-range dynamic mic. The version we have here is the XLR-only version which means this mic uses an XLR cord which does NOT come with the mic. It also means that we'd need some sort of external audio interface. There are other versions of this mic with USB connections but they're both more expensive and lower quality (XLR is higher quality than USB). This version of the mic also doesn't come with a stand. That being said if this isn't the last mic you intend to buy, an XLR cable and audio interface may be things you want to get regardless. While the USB options are more expensive, they're also more convient and for things like streaming it would be good enough quality.

Price: $180 (XLR version; doesn't include cost of cord, interface, or stand)

Shure SM7B

This is easily the most famous podcast microphone. The biggest issue with this microphone is that it requires a "preamp" or a high-end audio interface. A preamps could range from $50 to $2000 however, they do sell a $500 version called the SM7dB that comes with a built in preamp.

Price: $400 (doesn't include preamp)


Condenser Microphones

Samson Meteor

This microphone is our most budget friendly option. This is a USB-only microphone which means it often has lower quality, however this microphone is surprisingly really good. This is the perfect entry level mic for beginners. For things like streaming this could be the only mic you need, but if you're looking to make music or a podcast, you might want to look into a better mic down the line.

Price: $25

Audio-Technica AT2020

This is an XLR microphone, meaning it requires an audio interface to get it working. That being said this microphone has been a staple since its creation in 2004. If you watch a lot YouTubers or streamers you've almost definitely seen this microphone more than once.

Price: $100

Rode NT1 Signature Series

This microphone isn't a huge price change from the last. Microphones are often created for a specialized purpose. A mic like this would be a bit better for certain things over the AT2020, but to the average listener there wouldn't be a big difference if any. You would want this mic over the AT2020 if you were recording music professionally.

Price: $159


Shotgun Microphones

Takstar SGC-600

This budget shotgun mic is extremely good for the price. The biggest issue with this microphone lies in the threshold. Users have reported many issues with background noise. This microphone would be best for things like vlogging as it works best when you're around a foot away.

Price: $45

Rode VideoMicro 2

This is another fairly cheap option when it comes to shotgun mics. This mic isn't as good at cancelling out echo so either being outside or in a dampened room is best. This mic is also connected via USB-C, meaning it's rather simple to hook up to a phone.

Price: $80

Sennheiser MKE 600

This microphone offers high quality with good noise selection/cancellation. This is an XLR mic however it comes with an XLR to 3.5mm connector. That means this high end mic could be hooked up to a normal phone (if the phone has no 3.5mm jack an adapter would work well).

Price: $285


Lavalier Microphones

Boya BY-M1

This microphone is the best price to quality ratio we were able to find. This lav mic is compatible with phones, cameras, and computers using a 3.5mm jack. This bundle comes with a lapel clip, foam windscreen, and a 3.5mm to 6.3mm adapter. The only downside of this bundle is that it only comes with 1 lavalier mic. This may be okay for your purposes but most retailers sell their mics in packs of 2. That being said $24 for 2 mics is still a good deal.

Price: $12

Hollyland Lark M2

This bundle comes with 2 lavalier mics that have an advertised 30 hour battery life. These mics are smaller than most wireless lav mics on the market. These are great for phones but they also have compatibility with cameras using 3.5mm jacks. The different versions of this product correspond to their connection type. The cheapest is the USB-C(linked), then is the lightning cable, then the 3.5mm jack These options are sold seperately or in a combo. If you plan to use them with more than 1 connection the bundle for $20-$30 extra is a steal.

Price: $92

Rode Wireless Pro

These 2 offer over 40 hours of recording. These mics have universal compatibility with cameras and smartphones. These also have the furthest range out of any of the lav mics we've looked at.

Price: $360-$400
