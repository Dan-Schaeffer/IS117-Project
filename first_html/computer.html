Computers

Useful Information

When purchasing a computer the first step is identifying what you'll use the computer for. Things like writing or coding don't require things like a GPU while things like gaming or video editing almost require a GPU. One must also decide if they'll buy a laptop or a PC, if they decide to buy a PC they must also decide if they're going to build their PC or buy one pre-built. Pre-built computers are often more expensive but provide an out of the box experience. Custom PCs are often cheaper and more upgradable, but building a PC can be rather difficult for some. Either way it's important to know the important parts of a computer so you know what to look for when shopping.


The most important components for a computer when looking at content creation are a good to decent CPU, a dedicated GPU, and lots of storage.


Parts of a PC

CPU

A Central Processing Unit or CPU is essentially the brain of your computer. On a basic level a CPU runs the calculations and processes for your computer. A faster CPU means a faster PC, a faster PC means less frustration. It also means faster task completion, improved multitasking, smoother gaming, and editing. The measures of a good CPU lie in it's clock speed and core count.

The most popular brands are Intel and the AMD Ryzen series.

GPU

Your GPU or Graphics Processing Unit is what's responsible for rendering images and videos. GPUs are essential for things like gaming and video editing. When editing photos or videos a bad GPU can lead to slow rendering, laggy previews, and crashes. When gaming a bad GPU will lead to lower frames per second, reduced graphics quality, longer loading times, overheating, and crashes. A good graphics card is measured by its core count, clock speed, large and fast VRAM.

The most popular brands are NVIDIA with their GTX and RTX series, and AMD for their Radeon RX series.

RAM

RAM or Random Access Memory is temporary memory that stores data and programs for quick access. More RAM will help you when editing by providing faster previews, better multitasking, and fewer crashes. More RAM will also provide smoother gaming, shorter load times, and will cause less studdering when running background softwares. There are a lot of good brands when looking for RAM, 2 reliable retailers are Kingston and Corsair. We would recommed you get no less than 16GB of RAM, 32GB is ideal when editing with lots of effects.

Storage

Your long term storage is where data, programs, and your operating system will reside. There are 2 types of long term storage: a Hard Drive and an SSD. The difference between these 2 is speed. An SSD is much faster but often more expensive or with less storage. Hard Drives are usually cheaper and offer much more storage. If it's within your budget we would recommend having both an SSD and a Hard Drive. We'd recommend getting atleast 1TB worth of storage. You can also get an external hard drive for things like footage.

Mother Board

If a CPU is the brain of a PC, then a motherboard is the spine. The motherboard is what connects every component of the PC together. Without a motherboard you would simply have a bunch of computer parts with no use. The most important thing when looking at a motherboard is it's compatibility with the parts you intend to use. Things like the CPU socket, RAM support, GPU slots, and I/O ports are important to check before commiting to a motherboard. We recommend selecting your motherboard last so you know what connection types you require.

Power Supply Unit

A Power Supply or PSU is what gives your computer power. Without a power supply there's no way to plug your computer in. It's important to check that your power supply will be able to power all the components of your PC. We would recommend getting no less than 650W, a higher end PC would need 750W-1000W.

Input/Output

Your I/O would be things that plug into your computer. Input would be things like a mouse and keyboard. Output would be things like a monitor and headphones. These are not that important as they are easily upgradeable. While they don't determine the performance of your computer, they may limit your computers potential. For example, if you have the ability to play games at high frames, but if your monitor has a low refresh rate you won't be able to access those high frames.
