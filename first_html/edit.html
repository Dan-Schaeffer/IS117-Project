Editing

Useful Information

When creating content what you see is often not the raw files recorded. Everything gets edited from photos, to videos, to audio. We've already covered a bit about audio editing on our Music page. Below we will cover some good programs used for editing photos and videos.


Photo Editing

Adobe Photoshop

Photoshop is THE photo editing app. Photoshop is used for advanced photo editing, digital art, graphic desgin, and really anything visual you can think of. Photoshop is the industry standard for professional photo and design work. The learning curve can seem intimidating, but once mastered Photoshop is an incredibly powerful tool. A monthly charge may seem daunting, but there's also a bundle deal with all Adobe apps for $35.99/mo.

Price: $22.99/mo

Adobe Lightroom

Lightroom is used for organizing and editing large batches of photos. Lightroom is especially useful for photographers. Lightroom allows for non-destructive editing, so things like color correction, exposure, cropping, and more are easily done in light room. Lightroom allows users to edit many photos with a consistent style. A monthly charge may seem daunting, but there's also a bundle deal with all Adobe apps for $35.99/mo.

Price: $11.99/mo

GIMP

GIMP is a customizable, open source photo editing software. GIMP is meant to be an alternative to Photoshop however it doesn't do some things as good. GIMPs UI is a bit outdated, it can also lag with large files. GIMP has its limitations meaning that it isn't really seen in professional work, but it is a great entry point for users new to photo editing.

Price: Free


Video Editing

Adobe Premiere Pro

Adobe Premiere Pro is one of the most famous and widely used editing software. Premiere is fairly advanced meaning the learning curve can be pretty steep. Premier is widely used among professionals for its 4K,6K,8K+ capability and it's integration with Photoshop and After Effects.

Price: $22.99/mo

Adobe After Effects

Adobe After Effects is best known for its motion graphics and visual effects. Things like animations, effects, and compositing are available with After Effects. After Effects is extremely complex meaning it's mainly only used by professionals.


Price: $22.99/mo

Vegas Pro

Vegas Pro is a professional grade editing software. Vegas Pro is a good alternative to Premiere Pro especially for independent creators. Many people prefer Vegas Pro because it offers a one-time-purchase license as well as a monthly license.


Price: $19.99/mo or $179.00

DaVinci Resolve

DaVinci Resolve is a powerful video editing software that offers best-in-class color grading, VFX, and rendering all in one app. The downsides of DaVinci Resolve are the steep learning curve, heavy system usage, advance features locked behind paywall. There is a $295 Studio version, but the free version still offers many advanced features.

Price: Free
